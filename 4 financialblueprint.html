Financial Blueprint

We are seeking an initial $3.4 million from investors to fund Phase 1, which secures the land and core infrastructure. Comprehensive Repayment & Exit Strategy

A multi-pronged approach ensures investors see both near-term cash flow and long-term capital appreciation.

Cash Flow Distributions

Starting Year 1, annual profit distributions begin, providing immediate returns.

Recapitalization

A planned refinance in Years 5-7 aims to return a significant portion of initial capital.

Long-Term Appreciation

Investors retain their equity stake to benefit from the asset's compounding value.

Strategic Exit

A potential sale after Year 10+ provides a final exit to realize full capital gains.

The conventional debt is serviced directly from the project's operational revenue, a standard and secure structure.

Repayment Source

Debt service is paid from the Net Operating Income (NOI) generated by the property.

Amortization

The loan is projected to be fully paid off over a standard 20-year term.

Security

Secured by a first-lien position on all property and assets, ensuring maximum lender protection.

Permanent Financing

The construction loan will convert to a permanent mortgage post-stabilization.

We will actively pursue non-repayable funds to enhance project value and reduce the overall capital requirement.

Non-Dilutive

Grants are not loans and do not require repayment or dilute investor equity.

Target Areas

Focus on renewable energy, sustainable agriculture, community arts, and rural development.

Risk Reduction

Grant funding reduces the required amount of debt and/or equity capital.

Enhanced Returns

By reducing the capital base, grants directly increase the IRR for equity investors.
