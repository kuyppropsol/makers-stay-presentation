A Crisis-Proof Economic Model

The Maker's Stay is engineered for resilience through a multi-layered framework of legal, financial, and operational redundancies. This design provides profound stability, transforming a private venture into a foundational community asset that can weather economic volatility.

The Community Land Trust (CLT) Model

A Community Land Trust is a nonprofit, community-based organization designed to ensure long-term housing affordability and community stewardship of land. The CLT acquires land and holds it in trust for the benefit of the community, separating the ownership of the land from the ownership of the buildings on it. This innovative model allows us to create affordable homeownership opportunities through Kuyper Property Solutions while preserving the land as a community asset in perpetuity.

CLT vs. Traditional HOA

Our CLT-like Model

  • → Community Ownership of Land: The land is held in trust, removing it from the speculative market.
  • → Focus on Stewardship: Prioritizes long-term ecological health and community benefit.
  • → Permanent Affordability: Homeowners own their homes, but the trust owns the land, ensuring resale prices remain affordable.
  • → Democratic Governance: A board structure that includes residents and community members ensures decisions serve the collective good.

Traditional HOA Model

  • → Private Ownership of Land: Each homeowner owns their lot, subject to market forces.
  • → Focus on Rule Enforcement: Primarily concerned with maintaining property values through covenants.
  • → Market-Rate Fluctuation: Home prices are subject to the volatility of the real estate market.
  • → Board-Centric Governance: Typically governed by a board of homeowners focused on property management.

Long-Term Community Equity

The CLT model ensures the land's appreciating value is retained as a permanent asset for the entire community.

Strategic Allocation of Community Profits

As the project matures, the allocation of profits generated by the CLT will evolve to meet the community's changing needs, shifting from foundational investment to direct resident benefit.

Foundation & Growth

Community Reinvestment (50%)

Aggressively funding new projects, infrastructure upgrades, and expansion of amenities to enhance community value and future revenue potential.

Stewardship Reserve (30%)

Building a robust fund for long-term land maintenance, ecological restoration projects, and unforeseen capital repairs.

Resident Dividends (10%)

Initiating early-stage profit sharing to residents, fostering a sense of ownership and shared success from the outset.

Programming Fund (10%)

Seeding the budget for educational workshops, community arts projects, and cultural events that enrich resident life.

Maturity & Stability

Community Reinvestment (35%)

Continued strategic investment in value-add projects based on community feedback, such as a natural swimming pond or expanded workshop facilities.

Stewardship Reserve (25%)

Maintaining a healthy reserve while actively deploying funds for ongoing stewardship initiatives like reforestation or waterway protection.

Resident Dividends (25%)

Significantly increasing direct financial returns to residents as the business stabilizes and profits grow.

Programming Fund (15%)

Expanding the scope and quality of community-focused programming, including bringing in guest instructors and artists.

Legacy & Abundance

Resident Dividends (40%)

The largest share of profits is now returned directly to the residents, reflecting a mature and highly profitable operation.

Community Reinvestment (20%)

Focusing on legacy projects and major, long-term enhancements, such as endowing a local scholarship or acquiring adjacent conservation land.

Stewardship Reserve (20%)

Ensuring the ecological and physical health of the land for generations to come through a self-sustaining endowment model.

Programming Fund (20%)

Establishing a permanent, self-sustaining endowment for a rich cultural and educational program without relying on annual revenue.

Financial & Operational Resilience

Diversified Revenue Streams

Revenue is generated from multiple sources, insulating the project from downturns in any single sector.

Low Break-Even Point

The model is designed for profitability even in conservative scenarios, providing a significant margin of safety.

Hard Asset Foundation

The core investment is in tangible assets (land and infrastructure), which hold intrinsic value and are less susceptible to market speculation than purely operational businesses.

  • Intrinsic Value: Unlike stocks or digital assets, land and buildings have inherent physical value that provides a stable financial floor.
  • Inflation Hedge: Real assets historically appreciate in value during inflationary periods, protecting invested capital from losing purchasing power.
  • Secure Collateral: The tangible nature of these assets allows for more favorable and secure financing terms for future phases and refinancing events.

Barter & Mutual Credit System

An internal economy allows residents and the business to exchange goods and services, reducing reliance on external cash flow and strengthening community interdependence.

  • Reduced Cash Dependency: Allows operations to continue smoothly even with tight external cash flow, as essential services can be exchanged internally.
  • Community Strengthening: Fosters collaboration, mutual support, and a deeper sense of shared purpose among residents and the business.
  • Economic Velocity: Keeps value circulating within the community, multiplying the economic impact of every transaction.
