My Lived Experience: The Grounding Force

For the first three decades of my life, I navigated the world with a sense of "wrongness" that I couldn't place. The misdiagnoses of ADHD and Bipolar 2 were not the full picture; they were symptoms of a system that didn't understand the complex intersection of my neurodivergence. This lifelong struggle is the very foundation of my vision. It gave me a profound empathy for the human struggle and the understanding that true resilience is not about surviving a crisis, but about building a life that is fundamentally crisis-proof. The Maker's Stay is a direct, constructive response to that existential crisis—a physical manifestation of the sanctuary I needed but never had.

From a "Special Interest" to a Strategic Advantage

A Lifelong Obsession with Stones

My lifelong, absorbing passion for stones, jewelry making, and lapidary arts was more than a hobby; it was a self-soothing mechanism, a constant, tactile search for calm in an overwhelming world. This deep-seated fascination with tangible, grounded systems is the direct inspiration for our core aesthetic and revenue stream. The Maker's Stay is built as a sensory sanctuary, with natural wood finishes, grotto-inspired bathrooms, and a fully equipped lapidary studio—a physical manifestation of a lifelong need.

+ The Therapeutic Power of Tactile Experience

My experience with stones is not unique to the neurodivergent community. The **act of creating with one's hands** is a powerful tool for self-regulation and emotional well-being. This is why our business model, which centers on **experiential education** and artisanal crafts, is so much more than a product—it is a purpose.

The Crisis-Proof Model: A Thesis of Resilience

The business model is not just financially sound; it is a profound declaration that a new economic paradigm is not only possible but is already being built. Our unique legal structure and the strategic choice of location are the final pieces of this crisis-proof blueprint.

The Power of Collective Security

The **Community Land Trust (CLT)** model empowers residents by decoupling land from the speculative market. It fosters a high-trust environment where members are directly involved in governance, enabling mutual aid networks and barter systems to flourish in a crisis. This is a crucial layer of security that traditional models lack.

A Focus on Market Value

A traditional Homeowners Association is a market-driven model designed to preserve and enhance property values. While this can work in stable times, it is inherently vulnerable to market speculation and financial shocks. The focus is on individual equity and control, not on collective resilience.
