The "Crisis-Proof" Model: A Framework for Resilience

The Maker's Stay is engineered from the ground up to withstand systemic shocks. Our multi-layered legal, financial, and operational frameworks insulate the project from the vulnerabilities that could cripple conventional enterprises. This is not an accident; it is a deliberate strategy to build a long-term, sustainable community asset.

Legal Resilience: The Maker's Trust

The cornerstone of our model is a hybrid legal structure, The Maker's Trust, which functions similarly to a Community Land Trust (CLT). In this model, the Trust retains ownership of the land in perpetuity, while residents and businesses hold long-term, inheritable leases. This structure fundamentally disconnects the value of the land from the speculative real estate market, providing a powerful layer of security against market downturns and gentrification.

Defining the Community Land Trust (CLT) Model

For those unfamiliar with the structure, a Community Land Trust is a nonprofit, community-based organization designed to ensure permanent community stewardship of land. Hereâ€™s how it works:

  • Dual Ownership Structure: The CLT acquires land and holds it in trust for the benefit of the community forever. It then sells or leases the buildings on that land (like homes and businesses) to residents at affordable prices.
  • Long-Term Leases: An individual or family who buys a home on CLT land enters into a long-term, inheritable ground lease with the trust, typically for 99 years. This lease grants them exclusive use of the land and full ownership of their home.
  • Preserving Affordability: The ground lease includes a resale formula that determines the home's sale price in the future. This allows homeowners to earn a fair, but limited, amount of equity upon selling, while ensuring the home remains affordable for the next income-qualified buyer. This is the key to creating a permanent stock of affordable housing.
  • Community Control: CLTs are governed by a board often composed of a mix of leaseholders, community members, and public representatives. This democratic structure ensures the organization remains accountable to its mission and the people it serves.

CLT-like Model vs. Traditional HOA Model

The Maker's Trust (CLT-like Model)

Primary Goal

Mission-driven: to build a stable, resilient, and self-sustaining community for the long term.

Land Ownership

The trust retains ownership of the land in perpetuity. Residents hold long-term leases.

Resilience & Security

Proven to provide a critical layer of security against systemic shocks and market downturns. Protects against foreclosure and displacement.

Governance

Democratic and mission-focused, with representation from residents, the community, and other stakeholders.

Traditional HOA Model

Primary Goal

Market-driven: to protect and enhance individual property values.

Land Ownership

Individual homeowners hold ownership of both their house and land.

Resilience & Security

Vulnerable to speculative market forces and foreclosures. Can accelerate displacement during economic crises.

Governance

Typically governed by a board of directors elected by property owners, focused on property value.

Financial & Operational Resilience

Beyond the legal framework, our model is fortified by a multi-pronged financial and operational strategy designed for stability and adaptability.

Strategic Phased Plan

By completing foundational infrastructure in Phase 1, we create a tangible, valuable asset. This de-risks the project and secures favorable financing for subsequent phases, building a strong foundation from day one.

Diversified Revenue Streams

Our hybrid model of hospitality, real estate, and skills-based workshops creates multiple, uncorrelated income sources that buffer against market-specific downturns.

All-Weather Stability

If tourism dips, our long-term residential leases and workshop memberships provide a stable, predictable income base. This operational diversification is key to our ability to thrive in any economic climate.
