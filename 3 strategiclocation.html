Strategic Location: The Upper Peninsula

The choice of Michigan's Upper Peninsula (U.P.) is not an incidental detail but a cornerstone of our strategic plan. The region's unique confluence of economic trends, cultural identity, and natural assets makes it a premier, de-risked location for a venture like The Maker's Stay.

Economic & Market Strength

  • → Robust Tourism: A top-performing market with a thriving four-season tourism economy ensures consistent demand.
  • → "Maker" Culture: A deep-rooted, flourishing artisan and maker culture provides a rich talent pool and authentic local experiences.
  • → Supportive Government: State and local policies actively support agritourism, land preservation, and small business development.

Unparalleled Natural Assets

  • → Vast Wilderness: Millions of acres of national forests, hundreds of miles of shoreline, and pristine natural beauty.
  • → Outdoor Recreation Hub: Premier destination for hiking, kayaking, skiing, and snowmobiling, attracting a diverse visitor base.

Deep Community Alignment & Mutual Benefit

Economic Synergy

We create high-quality, year-round jobs, source materials and services from local suppliers, and attract tourism revenue that circulates within the local economy. Our model is designed to be a catalyst for, not a drain on, regional prosperity.

Cultural Preservation

The project celebrates and provides a platform for the U.P.'s rich artisan and "maker" culture. Our workshops and community spaces are designed to be resources for local artists and creators, preserving and promoting the region's unique identity.

Shared Infrastructure Goals

Our investment in renewable energy, resilient infrastructure, and sustainable land management aligns with the long-term goals of local governments focused on smart, sustainable development and environmental stewardship.

Community Asset Building

The Maker's Stay is not an isolated retreat. Through our CLT-like model and community-focused amenities, we are building a long-term asset that provides value and stability for residents and the broader community, not just for transient guests.
